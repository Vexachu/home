Why Code?

Coding is how we talk to computers to make things: games, stories, apps, robots, even music and art.

  • Create: build your own ideas, not just play them.
  • Think: break big problems into tiny steps (this is called computational thinking).
  • Share: show family & friends what you made on the web.

Mini‑Assignment

Open a new doc and write 3–5 sentences answering:

  1. What would you like to make with code?
  2. Who would enjoy your project (you, friends, little kids, everyone)?
  3. What skills do you hope to learn this year?

Teacher tip: Let students share 1 idea out loud. Celebrate any idea, even silly ones!

Why Scratch?

We’ll start with Scratch because it’s perfect for beginners:

  • Block coding: snap pieces together—no semicolons!
  • Visual: see characters move, draw, and talk right away.
  • Safe sharing: show projects to others and remix.
